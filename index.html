Course Description

This is a summer course designed to give high school students an opportunity to learn about computer science in San Leandro. The course will use Python as the programming language and follow three modules involving graphics programming with Processing, data processing with scientific libraries (matplotlib, pandas, numpy), and modeling the real world. By the end, students will have a taste of what programming is like, so they can make their own informed choices about pursuing computer science in the future.

Who This Program Is For

We aim to help students in San Leandro high schools starting with San Leandro High School and KIPP King, but others are also welcome to apply. No programming experience is necessary join. We hope to provide a curriculum that is open to all introductory students.

Motivation

Herrick: At San Leandro High School, I took my first CS course in 2013. It was terrible! Instead of learning, my class played Tetris and card games. This created my initial negative impression of CS. However, my impression changed when I went to college. I found the subject to be exciting, practical for helping people, and useful for scaling ideas. By creating this course, I hope to provide a way to help San Leandro kids get an earlier start in tech.

Currently, there are not many opportunities to pursue computer science in San Leandro as a secondary school student. So, our motivation is to provide a good opportunity for young students to learn CS from people who have experienced the field and are optimistic about the future of tech.

Worksheets

Worksheets will be provided online each week before class, so students have a reference about topics that will be covered. The goal of the worksheets is to provide exercises that will enhance practical skills.

Technology

Preferably, students will bring their own laptops, so that they can spend extra time outside of the weekly sessions to continue their learning. However, most students may not have access to laptops, so we will do our best to provide the necessary equipment each week.
